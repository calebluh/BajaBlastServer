🏜️ Scorchful: Desert & Heat Survival Guide
"Survival is the ability to swim in strange water."

Scorchful completely overhauls survival in warm biomes (Deserts, Badlands, Jungles) and The Nether by introducing a punishing temperature and heatstroke system. If you aren't prepared for the elements, the environment will kill you before the mobs do.

🌡️ 1. The Temperature System
Your body temperature is now tracked via a burning heart overlay on your health bar. Temperature is measured on a scale from 0 to 6,300.

How You Accumulate Heat:

Passive Heating (Overworld): Standing in direct sunlight during the day in warm or scorching biomes.

Passive Heating (Nether): The ambient heat is intense. Standing near the Lava Ocean or near bright artificial light sources (block light level 13+) will drastically raise your temperature.

Active Heating: Being on fire adds massive amounts of heat instantly (24 points/tick). Taking a hit from a Ghast or Blaze fireball adds a flat 1,000 heat points. Being submerged in lava also rapidly cooks you.

The Consequences of Overheating:
As your temperature gauge fills, you will suffer escalating debuffs:

50% Heat: Weakness I and audible heartbeat sounds.

75% Heat: Mining Fatigue I, Hunger I, and rapid heartbeats.

99%+ Heat: Weakness II, Hunger II, Heat Stroke (hallucinations, vision blur, and screen wobble), and taking 1 point of overheating damage every second.

💦 2. Cooling Down & Staying Wet
To survive the heat, you must manage your "Wetness" level (tracked via a checkerboard pattern behind your health and water drip particles). Wetness drains heat from your body.

Direct Soaking: Swimming, standing in the rain, throwing a Splash Potion on yourself, or stepping on a Nether Crimson Lily will instantly soak you.

Sweating: If you drink water (from a Water Skin, Cactus Juice, Water Bottles, or hydrating foods like Farmer's Delight items), you build up "Body Water." Your body will automatically consume this water to produce sweat, cooling you down over time.

The Humidity Factor: Cooling is drastically affected by biome humidity. In dry climates (like Deserts), sweating and wetness cool you down twice as fast. In highly humid climates (like Jungles or during rainstorms), the moisture in the air prevents sweat from evaporating, making cooling incredibly slow and difficult (simulating real-world wet-bulb temperatures).

🌪️ 3. Environmental Hazards & Armor
Sandstorms: During weather events in Deserts and Badlands, massive sandstorms will roll in. They reduce visibility but block the sun's heat. Place cauldrons outside during a sandstorm to collect renewable sand!

Armor Penalties: Wearing heavy armor (like Iron or Diamond) traps heat against your body, making you overheat significantly faster. You will need to seek out specialized gear like Turtle Armor, the Sun Hat, or Netherite Armor (which has innate heat resistance) to survive the harshest climates.

🦇 4. The "Fear" Effect
This mod introduces a new status effect called Fear (brewed using Redstone Dust and unknown base potions).

On Mobs: Causes them to stop attacking and flee from their targets.

On Players: Darkens and desaturates your screen, while simultaneously doubling the detection range of hostile mobs (making them able to track you through walls).
